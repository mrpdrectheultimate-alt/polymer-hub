## What is a Gate?

A **gate** is the restricted opening through which molten plastic flows from the runner system into the mould cavity. It is the final control point before the melt fills the part.

Gate design affects:
- **Fill pattern** — how the melt flows through the cavity
- **Weld line location** — where two flow fronts meet
- **Part appearance** — visible gate mark after ejection
- **Residual stress** — stress frozen into the part
- **Cycle time** — gate freeze-off determines holding time

---

## The Gating System: Overview

```
SPRUE → RUNNER → GATE → CAVITY
```

- **Sprue:** Main channel from nozzle into the mould
- **Runner:** Distributes melt to multiple cavities
- **Gate:** Restricted entry into each cavity
- **Cavity:** Final part shape

---

## Types of Gates

### 1. Edge Gate (Side Gate)
- Located on the parting line, at the edge of the part
- Most common, easiest to machine
- Leaves visible gate mark requiring trimming
- **Used for:** flat or simple parts — boxes, lids, trays

### 2. Submarine Gate (Tunnel Gate)
- Gates below parting line through angled tunnel
- **Auto-degating:** gate breaks automatically during ejection
- No visible gate mark on part surface
- **Used for:** consumer products where appearance matters — caps, cosmetic parts

### 3. Pin Gate (Hot Runner / 3-Plate)
- Small diameter gate (~0.5–1.5mm) from top of part
- Used in 3-plate moulds or hot runner systems
- Minimal gate mark, good for centre-gating round parts
- **Used for:** round containers, caps, medical parts

### 4. Fan Gate
- Wide, thin gate spread across part edge
- Distributes flow evenly, reduces warpage in flat parts
- **Used for:** flat panels, sheets, lenses

### 5. Film Gate (Flash Gate)
- Extremely thin, wide gate along entire edge
- Very uniform fill, virtually no warpage
- **Used for:** thin flat parts, optical components

### 6. Diaphragm Gate
- Ring-shaped gate for cylindrical parts
- Eliminates weld lines completely
- **Used for:** pipes, bushings, cylindrical containers

### 7. Hot Tip Gate
- Part of hot runner system; gate remains molten
- No cold slug, no runner waste
- Higher tooling cost but zero material waste
- **Used for:** high-volume production — bottle caps, closures

## Gate Sizing Rules

**Gate thickness** = 50–75% of wall thickness at gate location

**Gate width:**
- For edge gate: 1–2× wall thickness
- For fan gate: spread over 25–50mm

**Too small gate:**
- High shear heating → material degradation
- Premature gate freeze → sink marks

**Too large gate:**
- Difficult to trim, large gate scar
- Slow gate freeze → longer cycle time

---

## Gate Location Principles

1. **Place gate at thickest section** — melt flows from thick to thin, reducing sink marks
2. **Avoid gating opposite a core pin** — causes jetting and weld lines
3. **Gate away from functional surfaces** — gate mark should not appear on visible or mating surfaces
4. **For long parts: gate at one end** — ensures unidirectional flow, predictable orientation
5. **Multiple gates for large parts** — reduces fill pressure but creates weld lines

---

## Weld Lines and Gate Position

Weld lines form where two melt fronts meet. They are:
- Weaker than the base material (10–20% strength reduction)
- Visible as lines on part surface
- Unavoidable with multiple gates or flow around holes

**To control weld lines:**
- Position gates so weld lines occur in low-stress areas
- Use flow simulation (Moldflow / SolidWorks Plastics) before cutting the mould

### Indian Industry Example

**Greaves Cotton** and **Minda Industries** (automotive plastics, Pune) use **hot runner moulds with valve gates** for high-volume automotive parts like dashboard trim panels and bumper components.

**Ferromatik Milacron India** (Ahmedabad) — one of India's largest injection moulding machine manufacturers — trains toolmakers specifically on gate design as part of their **DGT (Die and Tool) certification programme**.

For packaging, **Manjushree Technopack** (Bengaluru) — which makes PET preforms for Coca-Cola and Pepsi India — uses **hot tip pin gates** in 96-cavity moulds for bottle preforms, producing millions of preforms daily with zero gate waste.

---

## Key Takeaways

- The gate is the most critical design element in a mould — it controls fill pattern, appearance, cycle time, and weld line location
- Submarine and hot runner gates give clean, gate-mark-free parts for appearance-critical products; edge gates are cheapest for simple parts
- Gate size must balance easy fill (larger) against short cycle time and clean trim (smaller) — 50–75% of wall thickness is the starting point
