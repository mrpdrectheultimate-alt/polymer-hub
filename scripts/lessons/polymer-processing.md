## What is Injection Moulding?

**Injection moulding** is a manufacturing process where molten plastic is injected under high pressure into a closed metal mould, allowed to cool and solidify, then ejected as a finished part.

It is the **most widely used plastics processing method** in India, accounting for over 40% of all plastic products manufactured.

**Typical products:** chairs, buckets, crates, automotive dashboards, bottle caps, electrical switches, toys, medical syringes.

---

## The Injection Moulding Machine

An injection moulding machine has three main sections:

### 1. Injection Unit (Plasticating Unit)
- **Hopper:** Feeds plastic granules into the barrel
- **Barrel + Screw:** Rotating screw melts and conveys plastic forward
- **Heater bands:** Maintain melt temperature along barrel zones
- **Nozzle:** Connects barrel to mould gate

### 2. Mould Clamping Unit
- **Fixed platen:** Holds the stationary half of the mould
- **Moving platen:** Holds the ejector half, moves to open/close mould
- **Tie bars:** Guide the moving platen; resist injection pressure
- **Clamping force:** Measured in tonnes — keeps mould closed during injection

### 3. Control System
- PLC-based temperature, pressure, and timing controls
- Modern machines: servo-hydraulic or all-electric drives

---

## The Injection Moulding Cycle

The cycle has 4 stages:

```
1. MOULD CLOSE → platens clamp shut
2. INJECTION   → screw pushes melt into mould cavity under pressure
3. COOLING     → part solidifies; screw recovers for next shot
4. EJECTION    → mould opens; ejector pins push part out
```

**Cycle time** = Injection time + Cooling time + Mould open/close time

Cooling time is typically **60–70% of total cycle time** — biggest lever for productivity improvement.

## Key Processing Parameters

| Parameter | Typical Range | Effect |
|-----------|--------------|--------|
| Melt Temperature | 180–320°C (material dependent) | Affects flow, degradation |
| Mould Temperature | 20–120°C | Affects surface finish, crystallinity |
| Injection Pressure | 500–1500 bar | Fills cavity completely |
| Holding Pressure | 50–80% of injection pressure | Compensates for shrinkage |
| Injection Speed | 20–200 mm/s | Affects weld lines, surface |
| Cooling Time | 5–60 seconds | Determines cycle time |
| Back Pressure | 5–20 bar | Controls melt homogeneity |

## Common Defects and Causes

| Defect | Appearance | Cause | Fix |
|--------|-----------|-------|-----|
| Short Shot | Incomplete part | Low pressure/temp, blocked gate | Increase injection pressure or melt temp |
| Sink Marks | Depressions on surface | Insufficient holding pressure, thick walls | Increase holding pressure, reduce wall thickness |
| Weld Lines | Visible line on part | Two flow fronts meeting | Increase melt temp, injection speed |
| Flash | Thin fins at parting line | Excessive injection pressure, worn mould | Reduce pressure, check mould clamping |
| Warpage | Distorted part | Uneven cooling, improper gate location | Balance cooling channels, adjust gate position |
| Burn Marks | Black/brown discoloration | Trapped air, excessive speed | Add vents, reduce injection speed |
| Jetting | Snake-like pattern | High speed through narrow gate | Reduce speed, increase gate size |

### Indian Industry Example

**Supreme Industries Limited** (Pune) operates one of India's largest injection moulding facilities with over 800 machines producing storage containers, material handling crates, and furniture.

Their **Nilkamal** brand chairs are injection moulded from **PP Homopolymer (Repol grade by Reliance)**:
- Melt temp: 220–240°C
- Mould temp: 40°C (water cooled)
- Cycle time: ~45 seconds per chair
- Clamping force: 500 tonnes machine

**Sintex Industries** (Kalol, Gujarat) uses injection moulding for their HDPE water storage tanks — using large 2,000-tonne machines for single-piece tank production, eliminating weld seams that could leak.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Injection moulding has 4 stages: mould close → injection → cooling → ejection; cooling dominates cycle time
- The 4 critical parameters to control are melt temperature, injection pressure, holding pressure, and cooling time
- Most defects (sink marks, short shots, warpage) are caused by incorrect pressure or temperature settings and can be fixed by systematic parameter adjustment
