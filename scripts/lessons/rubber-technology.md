## What is Vulcanization?

**Vulcanization** is a chemical process that creates **cross-links** between polymer chains in rubber, converting it from a soft, tacky, weak material into a strong, elastic, dimensionally stable product.

Discovered by **Charles Goodyear** in 1839 — he accidentally dropped a rubber-sulphur mixture on a hot stove and found it became elastic and strong instead of melting.

**Before vulcanization:** Rubber is sticky, weak, dissolves in solvents, melts in heat, brittle in cold.
**After vulcanization:** Elastic, strong, resistant to heat and solvents, dimensionally stable.

---

## The Chemistry of Sulphur Vulcanization

Natural rubber (NR) is **polyisoprene** — a long chain with a double bond (C=C) in each repeating unit. Sulphur reacts with these double bonds to form **polysulphidic cross-links** between adjacent chains.

```
Chain 1 — CH₂ — C(CH₃) = CH — CH₂ —
                      |
                    S₁–S₄  (cross-link)
                      |
Chain 2 — CH₂ — C(CH₃) = CH — CH₂ —
```

**Types of cross-links formed:**
- **Polysulphidic (–Sx–, x = 3–6):** Flexible, good dynamic properties, but less heat stable
- **Disulphidic (–S₂–):** Balanced properties
- **Monosulphidic (–S–):** Heat stable, good compression set resistance

## Vulcanization Systems

### 1. Conventional Vulcanization (CV)
- **Sulphur:** 2.5–3.5 phr (parts per hundred rubber)
- **Accelerator:** 0.5–1.0 phr
- Produces mostly **polysulphidic cross-links**
- Good initial properties, poor heat ageing

### 2. Semi-Efficient Vulcanization (SEV)
- **Sulphur:** 1.0–1.7 phr
- **Accelerator:** 1.5–2.5 phr
- Mixed cross-link types
- Balanced properties — most common in industry

### 3. Efficient Vulcanization (EV)
- **Sulphur:** 0.3–0.8 phr (or sulphur donor)
- **Accelerator:** 3.0–5.0 phr
- Mostly **monosulphidic cross-links**
- Excellent heat resistance — used in tyre sidewalls, engine mounts

---

## Accelerators

Accelerators speed up vulcanization (reduce cure time from hours to minutes) and improve cross-link quality.

| Class | Example | Speed | Use |
|-------|---------|-------|-----|
| Thiazoles | MBT, MBTS | Slow-medium | General purpose NR |
| Sulphenamides | CBS, TBBS | Delayed action | Tyres, technical goods |
| Thiurams | TMTD, TETD | Ultra-fast | EV systems, latex |
| Dithiocarbamates | ZDEC, ZDBC | Very fast | Latex, low-temp cure |
| Guanidines | DPG | Slow | Secondary accelerator |

**Activator system:** ZnO (5 phr) + Stearic acid (2 phr) — essential for activating accelerators.

## The Vulcanization Curve (Rheometer Curve)

A **Moving Die Rheometer (MDR)** or **Oscillating Disc Rheometer (ODR)** measures torque vs time during cure, giving the vulcanization curve:

```
Torque
  |           _____________ Maximum torque (MH)
  |          /
  |         /
  |        /  ← Cure rate
  |_______/
  |  ts₁ t90
  |______________ Time
  
ts₁ = Scorch time (safety period before cure starts)
t90 = Optimum cure time (90% of full cure)
MH = Maximum torque (crosslink density)
ML = Minimum torque (compound viscosity)
```

**Key parameters:**
- **ts₁ (Scorch time):** Processing safety — time before premature vulcanization
- **t90 (Optimum cure time):** Time in mould at cure temperature
- **CRI (Cure Rate Index):** = 100 / (t90 – ts₁) — higher = faster cure

---

## Overcure and Undercure

- **Undercure:** Insufficient cross-links → weak, sticky, poor dimensional stability
- **Overcure (Reversion):** In NR, excess cure breaks down cross-links → loss of properties. Monitored by drop in torque after MH.

### Indian Industry Example

**MRF Limited** (Chennai) — India's largest tyre manufacturer — uses a sophisticated **EV (Efficient Vulcanization) system** for their tyre tread compounds:

- Compound: NR/BR blend (60:40 ratio)
- Sulphur: 1.5 phr (SEV system)
- Accelerator: CBS (1.2 phr) + TBBS (0.5 phr)
- ZnO: 5 phr, Stearic acid: 2 phr
- Cure temp: 160°C, t90: 18 minutes in hydraulic press

**Apollo Tyres** (Vadodara, Gujarat) and **CEAT Tyres** (Mumbai) follow similar vulcanization protocols, with compounds designed at their **R&D centres in Chennai and Bhandup** respectively.

**Rubfila International** (Thrissur, Kerala) — India's largest rubber thread manufacturer — uses **continuous vulcanization** for their latex-based rubber thread used in waistbands and medical gloves, employing a **hot water cure tunnel** at 95°C.

**RUBBERCON** India (organized by ATMA — Automotive Tyre Manufacturers Association) is India's premier rubber technology conference, where formulators present new vulcanization system research annually.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Vulcanization creates sulphur cross-links between rubber chains, transforming weak rubber into strong, elastic material; sulphur level and accelerator type control the cross-link type
- The three systems (CV, SEV, EV) differ in sulphur:accelerator ratio and produce different cross-link types with different heat stability
- The rheometer curve gives ts₁ (scorch safety) and t90 (mould time) — the two numbers every rubber technologist works with daily
