## What is Melt Flow Index (MFI)?

**Melt Flow Index** (also called Melt Flow Rate, MFR) is a measure of how easily a molten polymer flows under a standard load and temperature.

**Definition:** The mass of polymer (in grams) that flows through a standard die (2.095mm diameter, 8mm length) in 10 minutes under a specified load and temperature.

**Units:** g/10 min

**Standard:** IS 2267 (India) / ASTM D1238 / ISO 1133

---

## Why MFI Matters

MFI tells you two critical things:

1. **Processability:** Higher MFI = flows more easily = suitable for thin-wall injection moulding or high-speed film blowing
2. **Molecular Weight (indirect):** Higher MFI = lower molecular weight = softer, lower strength

```
High MFI → Low MW → Easy flow → Lower strength
Low MFI  → High MW → Hard flow → Higher strength
```

**Choosing the right MFI grade is one of the most important decisions a processing engineer makes.**

## MFI Test Procedure (IS 2267 / ASTM D1238)

### Equipment: Melt Flow Indexer (Extrusion Plastometer)

**Components:**
- Heated barrel (temperature controlled ±0.5°C)
- Standard die at barrel base
- Piston with interchangeable weights
- Cutting tool and stopwatch

### Step-by-Step Procedure:

1. **Preheat** the barrel to test temperature (e.g., 230°C for PP)
2. **Load** approximately 4–5g of dry polymer granules into barrel
3. **Insert piston** and apply preheating load for exactly **6 minutes**
4. **Apply test load** (e.g., 2.16 kg for PP standard test)
5. **Cut and discard** initial extrudate (30 seconds)
6. **Collect extrudate** at fixed time intervals (every 60 seconds)
7. **Weigh** each cut sample on analytical balance
8. **Calculate MFI** = (weight of sample × 600) / collection time in seconds

## Standard Test Conditions for Common Polymers

| Polymer | Temperature | Load | Typical MFI Range |
|---------|------------|------|-------------------|
| PP (Homopolymer) | 230°C | 2.16 kg | 2–35 g/10min |
| HDPE | 190°C | 2.16 kg | 0.1–20 g/10min |
| LDPE | 190°C | 2.16 kg | 0.2–25 g/10min |
| ABS | 220°C | 10 kg | 1–30 g/10min |
| PC | 300°C | 1.2 kg | 5–25 g/10min |
| PET | 285°C | 2.16 kg | 5–30 g/10min |
| Nylon 6 | 235°C | 2.16 kg | 5–50 g/10min |

## MFI vs Application

| MFI (PP) | Suitable Process | Indian Product Example |
|---------|-----------------|----------------------|
| 0.5–2 | Pipe extrusion | PPR pipes (Astral, Supreme) |
| 3–8 | Blow moulding | PP containers, jerrycans |
| 8–20 | Injection moulding | Chairs, crates, buckets |
| 20–35 | Thin-wall injection | Medical syringes, closures |
| 35+ | Fibres / meltblown | PP nonwoven fabric, masks |

## Common Errors in MFI Testing

- **Moisture in sample:** Causes bubbles in extrudate → falsely high MFI (always dry hygroscopic polymers like Nylon, PET before testing)
- **Temperature instability:** ±1°C error can change MFI by 5–10%
- **Incorrect die cleaning:** Previous material contamination → erratic results
- **Wrong load:** Using 5 kg instead of 2.16 kg doubles the apparent MFI

### Indian Industry Example

**Reliance Industries** prints MFI on every bag of polymer granules shipped. Their **Repol PP H030EG** grade (MFI 3.0 g/10min at 230°C/2.16 kg) is specifically designed for **pipe extrusion** at Astral Pipes and Supreme Industries.

Their **Repol PP H110MA** (MFI 11 g/10min) is used for **injection moulded crates** at Nilkamal and Sintex.

**CIPET Chennai, Ahmedabad, and Lucknow** conduct MFI testing as part of their **Polymer Quality Testing** certificate programme — one of the most employment-relevant skills for freshers entering QC roles at polymer processing companies.

**Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS)** mandates MFI testing under IS 14182 for PP pipes used in drinking water supply — ensuring pipes from companies like Finolex, Ashirvad, and Prince Pipes meet the 0.5–1.5 g/10min specification for pipe grade PP.

---

## Key Takeaways

- MFI measures flow rate (g/10 min) under standard conditions — higher MFI means easier flow but lower molecular weight and lower strength
- Each polymer has its own standard test temperature and load — always check the datasheet before testing
- MFI is the primary incoming QC test at every Indian polymer processing plant — mastering this test is directly employable knowledge
